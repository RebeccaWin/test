Work About Resume

Småtrollens Föräldrakooperativ

The Challenge.

To design a minimalistic mobile website for a parent cooperative named Småtrollen, that was more contemporary than their current website. And effortless to construct for a developer, while also being manageable and updatable for them in the long run.

The process.

Our process, as a Agile Team comprised of three UX-designers and a Scrum Master, was to design a prototype based on their specifications aswell as creating a user-friendly interface.

The goal.

A mobile website that radiated a welcoming atmosphere and reflected the amicable nature of their preschool that contained alot of pictures.

A website where the parents can see the amount of time the children at this preschool spend outdoors, engaging in exploration and play. As well as the level of importance placed on environmental initiatives.

1 Getting to know the client

Initially, we convened with the client to gain insight into their vision and ascertain their preferences for the proposed mobile website. During this consultation, we obtained a comprehensive understanding of the distinguishing features of their preschool, including their unwavering commitment, active environmental stewardship, community engagement, and emphasis on security.

The client expressed a desire for an informative, well- structured, visually appealing website that is accessible and sustainable over time. Additionally, they provided us with their current logo, several images of the preschool, the informative text and a color scheme that they wished to incorporate.

Their specifications included the need for a user-friendly website that can be easily updated, enabling modifications to both the text and images. Furthermore, they requested an interest form and a footer with contact details.

2Heuristic Evaluation

We conducted a Heuristic Evaluation before commencing work on their new mobile website. This process involves using rules of thumb to assess the usability of user interfaces. During the evaluation, we applied five of Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design to generate a heuristic report. The purpose of this evaluation was to identify potential usability issues in the existing website and utilize that knowledge to improve these aspects in the new website.

Specifically, when analyzing heuristic number 8 - Aesthetic & Minimalistic design - we observed inconsistencies in image placement and text formatting, making it challenging for users to comprehend information. Furthermore, there were instances of unrelated images being used on some pages.

Additionally, when examining heuristic number 4 - Consistency & Standards - we found that certain main headings had subheadings that duplicated their meaning, which deviates from standard practice on other websites. Moreover, the website employed eleven different colors, exceeding the recommended maximum of seven. This practice adds excess visual information for users, making it harder for them to process the content.

3Brainstorming

At the outset of the project, our team convened for a group workshop in which we utilized the Double-Diamond design process model. This model served as a means to discover various approaches to design the website layout and define the most effective ideas to lateron deliver Low-Fidelity wireframes.

Our team was dedicated to integrating all of the client's specifications and priorities, while emphasizing simplicity and ease of use as the primary objectives for an optimal user experience. We placed significant emphasis on ensuring that the user remained at the forefront of our design process.

4Design system

Before we began our prototype work, we crafted a comprehensive design system in Figma that incorporated various elements such as typeface, color scheme, icons, and buttons. To ensure consistency throughout the design and to to make the website more effortless to construct for a developer, we employed Bootstrap's icons, buttons, and components.

5Prototype

Then it was time to create the prototype for their new mobile website, the prototype was created in Figma and we used Bootstrap's 12 column grid in order to get a responsive and flexible layout. By utilizing heuristic evaluation and our design system, we successfully developed a prototype that meets the client's requirements and prioritizes the user-experience.

Color

To achieve a simplistic and minimalistic look, the new website design utilized only three colors in varying shades, with correct contrast ratios. The background color is off- white and the text is in a dark gray shade, providing a warmer feeling than a white background with black text. We couldn't incorporate the client's preferred color scheme due to poor contrast, but we did include a darker shade of green, which was also present in the original color scheme, to enhance contrast. The primary colors used were off-white and green, as they are associated with nature, harmony, and balance.

Text & logo

The website's text is left-aligned for optimal readability, and the font style used is Sans Serif, which is commonly used for websites due to its suitability. To improve readability, we have condensed the content, rephrased the text, and implemented bullet points. Symbolic images were added to the headings to bring a playful element to the website's appearance and create a welcoming atmosphere for both parents and children.  In terms of the logo, we made a few adjustments to enhance its readability. We switched to a bolder font since the previous one was too thin, and changed the color from light green to a darker shade for better contrast with the offwhite background.

Menu & footer

The placement of the menu icon on the right side of the website follows the standard on other websites, where users expect to find this button. To make it clear and visible for users where they can find the essential "Contact" page, we created a separate button for it. Given that many users are new, it was important to ensure high visibility for this function, as it is likely the information they need most. Additionally, the client with families. A Call-to-Action button is always available in the footer of each page to guide users on what to do next and to prevent them from missing this important function. Clicking on this button takes users to the "Contact" page where they can register their interest in enrolling their child at the preschool.

6Presentation

The moment of truth arrived as we presented our two-week project, during which we pitched our idea to the client. During the presentation, we exhibited the prototype we had designed and explained how all our ideas and implementations were founded on research and studies within UX-design. At the outset of the presentation, we displayed a QR code that led to the prototype view in Figma, allowing the clients to preview the website and gauge its feel before we delved into the details.

As one of nine groups that had tackled the same project with diverse outcomes for the website, our main objective was to convey why our version was the most suitable for the client.

Lessons Learnt

The stress of completing the project in two weeks became evident and affected our work process in various ways. However, this stress also had a positive impact on the group as it forced us to improve our decision-making skills and be satisfied with our accomplishments. We learned to finish work segments without striving for perfection.

In hindsight, we realized that as a group, we should have created an overall schedule at the beginning of the project, with set deadlines for each segment, creating a timeline to follow. This would have further challenged us to finish each segment and move on to the next.

Additionally, I learned the importance of usability testing, which we didn't prioritize in this project due to time constraints. Allocating time for this would have given us valuable insights into how the website functioned in practice and provided us with external perspectives.
